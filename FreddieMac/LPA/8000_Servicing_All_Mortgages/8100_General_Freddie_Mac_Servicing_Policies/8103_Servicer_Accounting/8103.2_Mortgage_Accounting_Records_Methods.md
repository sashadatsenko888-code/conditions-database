# ## Overview
Section 8103.2 establishes the required accounting system capabilities, amortization methods, interest calculation rules, and delinquency relief reporting.

---

# ## (a) Mortgage Accounting Records

### (i) Permanent Records
Servicers must maintain **permanent accounting records** for each Mortgage including Freddie Mac’s **nine-digit loan number**.

### (ii) Accounting System Requirements
System must provide chronological transcript of:

- Date/amount of each payment (interest, principal, negative amortization)
- Date interest is paid to
- All disbursements, advances, adjustments
- Current principal balance
- Current escrow balance
- Any escrow insufficiency

### (iii) Accounting Principles
Records must follow **GAAP** and allow Freddie Mac to audit at any time.

---

# ## (b) Accounting Methods

### (i) Amortization Method
Servicers must use **interest-in-arrears amortization**.

#### (A) Interest Calculation
- One full month's interest = principal × rate ÷ 12  
- Factors carried to six decimals, then rounded  
- Law requiring a different method → Servicer must notify Freddie Mac  

#### (B) Initial Interest℠ Mortgages
- IO payments until First Amortizing Payment Date  
- Must track Interest Change Date & First Amortizing Payment Date  

### (ii) Less-than-Full-Month Interest
For payoffs and third-party sales:  
**Actual days ÷ 365** method.

### (iii) Interest-in-Advance
Not permitted for Freddie Mac loans.  
Servicers using it must convert to **interest-in-arrears**.

---

# ## (c) Accounting for Delinquency Relief
- Apply borrower funds per Note or repayment agreement  
- Regardless of funds collected, Servicers must report using **net yield reporting**  
- Freddie Mac receives:
  - Proportional principal  
  - Accounting Net Yield interest
