Source: Freddie Mac Seller/Servicer Guide  
Section: **8102.2 – Obligations upon termination or expiration of Subservicing Agreement**  
Effective Date: **11/19/2025**

> **Condition-Relevant Section — Include in Ruleset**

---

## ## Overview
Section 8102.2 outlines requirements that apply when a Subservicing Agreement terminates or expires.  
It defines obligations of the Master Servicer, the prior Servicing Agent, and any new Servicing Agent, including due-diligence reviews, file transfers, reporting rules, borrower notification, and Freddie Mac oversight rights.

---

## ## (a) Servicing obligations undertaken by new Servicing Agent or Master Servicer
When a Subservicing Agreement ends and servicing transfers to a new Servicing Agent (or back to the Master Servicer), the Master Servicer and new Servicing Agent must submit the following **30 days before** the new Servicing Agent begins servicing:

- Fully executed **Form 479A**
- List of Mortgages included in the subservicing arrangement  
  (including Mortgages in loss-mitigation alternatives and terms of such alternatives)
- List of all Mortgages that will be serviced by the new Servicing Agent or directly by the Master Servicer

---

## ## (b) Mortgages modified electronically
If the portfolio includes electronically modified Mortgages:

- Provide a list of Mortgages with **eModification Agreements** or **Electronic Payment Deferral Agreements**
- Provide the **repository** holding these electronic documents

Freddie Mac indicates approval by returning the signed Form 479A.

---

## ## (c) Denied Form 479A requests
Freddie Mac may deny the Master Servicer’s request to:

- Use a new Servicing Agent, or  
- Perform servicing directly.

Freddie Mac may—but is not required to—provide written reasons.  
If denied, the Master Servicer must work with Freddie Mac to:

- Identify another Servicing Agent, or  
- Complete a Subsequent Transfer of Servicing under Chapter 7101.

---

## ## (d) Freddie Mac’s rights
Unauthorized servicing by a new Servicing Agent or Master Servicer is grounds for suspension or disqualification.  
Freddie Mac may also:

- Exercise any rights under the Purchase Documents  
- Conduct a **post-transfer review**, including onsite visits

---

## ## (e) Master Servicer and Servicing Agent reviews
The Master Servicer, prior Servicing Agent, and new Servicing Agent must jointly conduct due-diligence reviews, including:

- Mortgage files and servicing records  
- Custodial Accounts  
- Alternatives to foreclosure  
- Transfer data and documentation  

They must provide **all missing documents, data, and information** needed to perform servicing under Freddie Mac requirements.

---

## ## (f) Servicer to perform the servicing obligations
The Servicer undertaking new servicing responsibilities must:

- Perform its own due-diligence review  
- Review Custodial Accounts  
- Review obligations under Purchase Documents  
- Review alternatives to foreclosure  
- Review internal/external audits, including recent Freddie Mac audits

Upon request, Master Servicer and Servicing Agent must provide Freddie Mac with post-transfer review results.

---

## ## (g) Servicing reporting
Beginning with the first reporting cycle after transfer:

- **All servicing reports must be submitted under the Master Servicer’s Seller/Servicer number.**

---

## ## (h) Transfer of Mortgage and REO files
Within **30 days** of transfer, the prior Servicer must deliver:

### Mortgage File
- Complete Mortgage file per Chapter 3302  
- Convert or print documents if formats differ  
- For loans with Note Dates ≥ July 1, 2013 — use electronic scanning formats (PDF/TIF/JPEG)

### Payment History
- Complete payment and escrow disbursement history (including latest escrow analysis)

### Correspondence & Reports
- All borrower correspondence  
- All reports to FHA/VA/RHS/MI  
- All reports to Freddie Mac and government authorities

### Notice of Transfer
- Copy of borrower notice regarding servicing transfer

### REO (if applicable)
- Full REO history including expenditures, claims, and management documentation

---

## ## (i) Transfer of portfolio records
Delivered **no later than the date servicing transitions**:

- **Service contracts** (tax, flood)  
- **List of unpaid escrowed charges**  
- **Trial balances** showing:  
  - Transfers of Ownership, payoffs, exceptions  
  - Escrows, advances, prepayments  
  - Buydown balances  
  - Delinquencies, bankruptcies, REO  
- **Automatic draft list** with borrower authorization  
- **Insurance policy expirations**  
- **Ledger records and code definitions**  
- **Custodial Account reconciliations**  
- **Freddie Mac servicing & accounting reports for the prior 3 months**

---

## ## (j) Notices to the Borrower
The outgoing Servicer must notify Borrowers:

- At least **15 days before** the first payment due to the new Servicer  
- Notice must comply with law and ensure no payment disruption

---

## ## (k) Master Servicer / new Servicing Agent notice
The new Servicing Agent or Master Servicer must:

- Provide **written confirmation** of the transfer details  
- Send notice **at least 15 days before** the first payment due date

---

## ## (l) Notice requirements
Borrower notice must include:

- Effective transfer date  
- Name and address of new Servicer  
- Contact names & phone numbers for both old and new Servicers  
- Final/starting dates for payment collection  
- Instructions for automatic draft continuation  
- Clarification that the Mortgage terms are unchanged (except payment address)

The new Servicer must handle all borrower inquiries after the transfer.  
Late fees must be waived for misapplied payments caused by transition issues.

Outgoing Servicer must forward any received funds or correspondence **within 1 day**.
