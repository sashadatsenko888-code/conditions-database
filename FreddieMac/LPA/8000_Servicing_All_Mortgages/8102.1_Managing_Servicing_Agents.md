# ## Overview

Section 8102.1 establishes **Freddie Mac’s requirements for Master Servicers and Servicing Agents**, including approval rules, compliance responsibilities, oversight obligations, and termination/transfer requirements.

This section defines the relationship between a Master Servicer and its Servicing Agent, the limits of authority granted to the Servicing Agent, Freddie Mac’s enforcement rights, and reporting obligations.

---

# ## (a) Servicer’s and Servicing Agent’s Rights and Responsibilities

### **Servicer must:**
- Service Mortgages for Freddie Mac according to applicable law and Purchase Documents.  
- Service Government Mortgages (FHA, VA, RHS) in accordance with their rules; when conflicts exist, **government/MI rules control**.  
- If Seller ≠ Servicer → must execute **Concurrent Transfer of Servicing** per Chapter 7101.  
- Servicing Agent must be an **approved Freddie Mac Servicer** and comply with all Guide requirements.

### **Form 479A**
- Required for appointing a Servicing Agent.  
- Once executed & approved by Freddie Mac → becomes a Purchase Document.

### **Master Servicer + Servicing Agent agree that:**
- They are **jointly and severally liable** to Freddie Mac.  
- Freddie Mac may pursue either or both parties for violations (no double recovery).  
- Master Servicer retains full responsibility for all Guide requirements.  
- Servicing Agent is only authorized to access mortgage data for files it services.

### **Master Servicer obligations:**
- Must disclose all relevant Purchase Documents to the Servicing Agent.  
- Must maintain systems & oversight to ensure Servicing Agent complies with all requirements.  
- Guide always supersedes any conflicting provision in the Subservicing Agreement.  
- Servicing Agent has **no rights against Freddie Mac**, including:
  - Cannot assume Servicing Contract  
  - Cannot terminate or suspend Master Servicer  
  - Cannot transfer servicing to others  
  - Is **not** a third-party beneficiary  
  - Has **no ownership** of Servicing Contract Rights  

### **Freddie Mac’s rights include:**
- Suspend or terminate Master Servicer or Servicing Agent (with or without cause)  
- Transfer Servicing without regard to any rights of first offer/refusal in the Subservicing Agreement  

### **Reporting (by the 20th of each month):**
Master Servicer must provide:
- Notices of default under the Subservicing Agreement  
- Notices of potential defaults  
- Any additional information Freddie Mac requests  

Servicing Agent must also notify Freddie Mac of:
- Defaults  
- Potential defaults  

### **Freddie Mac access rights**
Master Servicer & Servicing Agent give Freddie Mac full rights to access:
- Mortgage records  
- Subservicing Agreements  
- Any data required to verify compliance

# ## (b) Requirements for Termination and Transfer of Subservicing Agreement

### **(i) Termination or Expiration**
- Master Servicer must notify Freddie Mac **within 1 Business Day** via new Form 479A.  
- Must specify whether:
  - Master Servicer will assume servicing, or  
  - A new Servicing Agent will be appointed (requires approval).

### **(ii) Suspension or Disqualification of Master Servicer**
- Subservicing Agreement **immediately terminates** without notice.  
- Rights such as right of first refusal immediately terminate.  
- Master Servicer & Servicing Agent must transfer all records as Freddie Mac instructs.  
- Freddie Mac may choose to contract directly with the Servicing Agent.

### **If the Servicing Agent is suspended or terminated**
- Subservicing Agreement immediately terminates.  
- Records must be transferred to Master Servicer or new approved Servicing Agent.

### **(iii) Transfer of records & funds**
- Must comply with Section 8102.2 requirements.

### **(iv) Master Servicer indemnification**
- Must indemnify Freddie Mac for losses caused by Servicing Agent’s:
  - Failure,  
  - Bankruptcy,  
  - Insolvency,  
  - Noncompliance.

---

# ## (c) Approval to Use Servicing Agents

- Only **one Servicing Agent per Seller/Servicer number** at a time.  
- Must obtain Freddie Mac written approval **before** Servicing Agent starts work.  
- Form 479A is mandatory.  
- Dormant relationships (no activity for 1 year) are automatically terminated.  
- eMortgage-specific rules apply (Section 1402.9(b)).

---

# ## (d) Review of Servicing Agent

Before submitting Form 479A, Master Servicer must review:
- Operational procedures  
- Methods of operation  
- Security standards (Sections 1401.5 & 1401.6)

Submitting Form 479A = warranty that Servicing Agent meets all requirements and has proven servicing ability.

---

# ## (e) Oversight and Surveillance

Master Servicer must establish a compliance oversight program.  
Best practices available at Freddie Mac’s official PDF.

---

# ## (f) Management Experience

Master Servicer must employ:
- Experienced mortgage servicing executive  
- Adequate staff based on portfolio volume and complexity

---

# ## (g) Outsourced Vendors

Freddie Mac does **not** consider Outsourced Vendors to be Servicing Agents.

Servicer must ensure vendors have:
- Compliant systems  
- Proper security (Ch. 1302)  
- Business continuity plan  
- Ability to provide accurate borrower responses  

Offshore vendors must meet **same standards** as U.S. vendors.

Servicer remains **fully responsible** for all vendor actions.
