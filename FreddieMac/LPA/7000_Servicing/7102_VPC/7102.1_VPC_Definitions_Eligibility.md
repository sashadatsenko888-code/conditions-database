## Overview
Establishes **definitions**, **eligibility**, and **consent conditions** for **Voluntary Partial Cancellation (VPC)** and **Flow VPC** Transfers of Servicing.  
These provisions govern the limited cases where Freddie Mac allows Servicers to **partially cancel** their Servicing Contract Rights under a specific Freddie Mac–approved agreement.

---

## (a) Key Terms and Definitions
The following definitions apply specifically to **Chapter 7102** and are as provided in:
- **Exhibit 30:** *Voluntary Partial Cancellation of Servicing Contract Rights Agreement (RPL & NPL) Incorporated Provisions*  
- **Exhibit 31:** *Flow Voluntary Partial Cancellation of Servicing Contract Rights Agreement Incorporated Provisions*

**Defined terms:**
- **Flow VPC Agreement**
- **Flow VPC Transfer(s) of Servicing**
- **VPC Agreement**
- **VPC Transfer(s) of Servicing**

> These definitions are binding contractual references incorporated directly into the respective agreements.

---

## (b) VPC Transfers of Servicing Eligibility
- Chapter 7102 **is not a general program** — participation is limited to **specific, pre-approved Servicers**.
- Chapter 7102 **does not apply** unless:
  - Freddie Mac and the Servicer **mutually execute and deliver** either:
    - a **VPC Agreement**, or  
    - a **Flow VPC Agreement**,  
  - and such agreement is **in form and substance acceptable to Freddie Mac**,  
  - determined **at Freddie Mac’s sole and absolute discretion**.

---

## (c) VPC and Flow VPC Transfer of Servicing Agreements

### (i) VPC Transfer of Servicing Consent and Agreement
- Freddie Mac’s consent to a VPC Transfer is **only effective upon execution** of a **VPC Agreement** by both parties.
- Each VPC Agreement must contain this mandatory language:

> “This VPC Agreement incorporates the provisions of Chapter 7102 and the provisions of Exhibit 30 by reference as a substantive contractual part of this VPC Agreement such that the Servicer expressly agrees that (i) Chapter 7102 is operative as to the Servicer and (ii) the Servicer is bound by the terms and conditions set forth in Chapter 7102 and Exhibit 30.”

### (ii) Flow VPC Transfer of Servicing Agreement Requirements
- Freddie Mac’s consent to a Flow VPC Transfer is **only effective upon execution** of a **Flow VPC Agreement** by both parties.
- Each Flow VPC Agreement must contain this mandatory language:

> “This Flow VPC Agreement incorporates the provisions of Chapter 7102 and the provisions of Exhibit 31 by reference as a substantive contractual part of this Flow VPC Agreement such that the Servicer expressly agrees that (i) Chapter 7102 is operative as to the Servicer and (ii) the Servicer is bound by the terms and conditions set forth in Chapter 7102 and Exhibit 31.”
