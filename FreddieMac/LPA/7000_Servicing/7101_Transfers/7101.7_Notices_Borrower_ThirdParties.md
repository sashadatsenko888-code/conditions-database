Section: 7101.7 – Required Notices to Borrowers and Third Parties; Borrower Issues and Inquiries Related to Transfers of Servicing  
Effective Date: 09/10/2025  

> **Condition-Relevant Section — Include in Ruleset**

---

## Overview
Outlines **notification duties** and **borrower-handling standards** when servicing rights are transferred.  
Specifies requirements for:
1. **Notices to Borrowers** (timing, contents, responsibilities).  
2. **Notices to Third Parties** (MI, insurers, agencies, associations).  
3. **Handling borrower inquiries, disputes, and transition issues**.

---

## (a) Notice to Borrowers Regarding Transfers of Servicing

### (i) Transferor Servicer’s Notice
- Must provide **written notice** to each Borrower **at least 15 days before** the first payment due to the **Transferee Servicer**.
- Purpose: ensure a **smooth transition**, prevent **payment disruption**, and ensure **legal compliance**.

### (ii) Transferee Servicer’s Notice
- Must send **written confirmation** mirroring the Transferor’s notice, **no later than 15 days before** the first payment due to them.

### (iii) Required Contents
Each notice must include:
1. **Effective Date of Transfer**.  
2. **Name and address** of the **Transferee Servicer**.  
3. **Names and phone numbers** of contact persons/departments at both Servicers for borrower inquiries.  
   - If toll-free numbers unavailable, must state that **collect calls are accepted**.  
4. **Date payment collection switches** from Transferor to Transferee.  
5. Disclosure of any **escrowed optional Mortgage life/accident/health insurance** that the Transferee will **not assume**, plus borrower instructions for continuation.  
6. **Automatic draft payment maintenance procedures**, if applicable.  
7. Prohibition: **Notice may not alter Mortgage terms**, except payment address or collection instructions.

---

## (b) Notices of Transfers of Servicing to Third Parties
**Transferor Servicer** must obtain/issue the following:

1. **MI approval & insurance commitment** per §7101.2(d)(i).  
2. Notify **property insurers** (including **FEMA**) of:
   - The **Transfer of Servicing**,  
   - The **Transferee’s name/address**,  
   - And the **Mortgage clause modification** per §4703.6.  
3. Provide required notices to **FHA, RHS, and/or VA**, if applicable.  
4. Notify **all other relevant third parties**, such as:
   - Mortgage life/accident/health insurers,  
   - Tax verification/reporting and flood zone determination services,  
   - **Tax authorities**,  
   - **HOAs, condo associations, co-ops** (see Ch. 8801 for Cooperative Share Loans),  
   - **Fee owners** (for leaseholds),  
   - **Other lienholders**,  
   - **Public utilities** or agencies levying assessments for which Escrow is collected.

---

## (c) Borrower Issues and Inquiries Related to Transfers of Servicing
- Both Servicers must ensure staff and systems can **handle accounting, payments, and borrower inquiries** during the transition.
- After the Effective Date:
  - The **Transferee Servicer** is responsible for **all borrower inquiries**.
  - If resolution requires Transferor input, **Transferee must initiate contact** (Borrower should not be tasked).
- During transition:
  - Both Servicers must **resolve borrower disputes** arising from **misunderstanding of transfer instructions**.
  - **Late charges must be waived** when caused by such confusion.
  - If payments were **misapplied/unapplied**, appropriate **payment/credit adjustments** must be made.
