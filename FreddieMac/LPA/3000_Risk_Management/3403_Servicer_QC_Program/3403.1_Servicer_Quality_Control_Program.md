## Overview

Section 3403.1 describes the **minimum standards** for a Servicer’s Quality Control (QC) program for the Servicing of Freddie Mac Mortgages.

The Servicer must:

- Maintain a written QC program  
- Monitor compliance consistently  
- Maintain strong internal controls  
- Ensure compliance with the Guide and all Purchase Documents  
- Perform reviews and take remedial action when deficiencies are found  
- Provide documentation and evidence of QC program effectiveness upon request  

Freddie Mac may require changes to the Servicer's QC program at any time.

---

## Core Requirements of the Servicer QC Program

### 1. **Written Policies & Procedures**
The Servicer must:

- Maintain **written QC policies**  
- Ensure procedures clearly outline responsibilities  
- Keep the QC program aligned with Freddie Mac Guide requirements

---

### 2. **Ongoing Monitoring and Risk Management**

The Servicer must:

- Monitor compliance with QC policies as part of a **prudent risk management framework**  
- Regularly review and assess:
  - Internal controls  
  - Procedures  
  - Systems used in Servicing Freddie Mac Mortgages  

Any deficiencies must be addressed promptly, regardless of who identifies them:

- The Servicer  
- Freddie Mac  
- A Borrower (if confirmed)  
- Third parties (e.g., auditor, law firm, regulator)

The Servicer must document all findings and remediation and provide these results to Freddie Mac upon request.

---

## Minimum Required Components of the QC Program

The QC program must include, at minimum:

### 1. **Fraud Prevention and Detection**
Must comply with:

- **3201.1(c)** – fraud detection and prevention  
- All relevant requirements under **Chapter 3201**

---

### 2. **Record Accuracy & Retention Control**

Servicer must evaluate compliance with:

- **Accurate and complete records** (Chapter 3302)  
- Document Custodian and retention requirements per:
  - 9206.4(b)  
  - 9205.1(b)  
  - 9205.3  

---

### 3. **Control & Identification of Mortgage Files**

Per **Section 3302.1(c)**:

- Mortgage files must have proper control features  
- Appropriate identification markers  
- Sufficient tracking for Servicing accuracy

---

### 4. **Periodic Operational Reviews**

Servicer must periodically test and review:

- **Cash accounting**  
- **Investor reporting & remitting**  
- **Escrow management**  
- **Borrower notifications**  
- **Custodial account management**

Sampling must:

- Use a meaningful representation of the **UPB** of Freddie Mac loans serviced

---

### 5. **ARM Adjustment Accuracy**

Servicer must:

- Assess accuracy of ARM adjustments  
- Verify Borrower notifications are correct and timely

---

### 6. **Outsourced Vendor QC Audits**

If an Outsourced Vendor uses any **Servicing Tool**, the Servicer must:

- Conduct periodic QC audits  
- Ensure compliance with the Guide and Purchase Documents  
- Confirm vendor compliance with **Section 2406.1**

---

### 7. **Preforeclosure File Review Compliance**

Servicer must evaluate compliance with:

- **9101.1(e)** – Preforeclosure referral file review  
- **9102.4(b)** – Preforeclosure referral requirements  
- **9301.2(a)** – Servicer’s review obligations  
- **9301.7(b)** – Preforeclosure sale account review

---

### 8. **Default Notices & Legal Compliance**

Periodic review must ensure:

- Default notices, acceleration notices, and cure notices are:
  - Properly dated  
  - Timely sent  
  - Legally compliant  
- All **conditions precedent to foreclosure** have been met

---

### 9. **Loss Mitigation Compliance**

Servicer must periodically review:

- Compliance with Freddie Mac’s loss mitigation requirements  
- Modification program requirements  
- Short sale requirements  

---

### 10. **Borrower Complaint/Error Resolution Procedures**

Servicer must maintain policies designed to ensure:

- Complaints and asserted errors are investigated  
- Appropriate responses are given  
- Corrections are made promptly
