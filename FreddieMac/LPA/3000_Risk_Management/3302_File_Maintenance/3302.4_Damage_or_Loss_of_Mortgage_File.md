Section: 3302.4 – Damage or Loss of Mortgage File  
Effective Date: 05/01/2020  

> **Condition-Relevant Section — Include in Ruleset**

---

## Overview
This section establishes the Servicer’s **full financial responsibility** for restoring any **damaged or lost Mortgage files** or related records.  
Freddie Mac requires Servicers to maintain adequate systems, backups, and disaster recovery controls to ensure file restoration without delay.

---

## Requirement Summary

1. **Servicer Responsibility**  
   - The Servicer **must bear 100% of the cost** of restoring Mortgage files, documents, or records that are:  
     - **Damaged**,  
     - **Destroyed**, or  
     - **Lost** for **any reason**, including:  
       - Fire, flood, or natural disaster,  
       - System failure or data corruption,  
       - Human error, or  
       - Unauthorized access or mishandling.  

2. **Prompt Restoration**  
   - Restoration must occur **immediately and at the Servicer’s expense**, without waiting for reimbursement or insurance recovery.  
   - The Servicer must use **original backups or verified duplicates** to reconstruct the files to their original completeness and legibility.

3. **Risk Controls (Implied Standard)**  
   - While not explicitly stated, this section assumes compliance with Freddie Mac’s expectations for:
     - **Secure off-site storage**,  
     - **Regular data backups**,  
     - **Fire-resistant and disaster-protected facilities**, and  
     - **Electronic redundancy systems** ensuring continuity of servicing documentation.
