## Overview
This section reinforces the **continuing legal and procedural obligations** of Seller/Servicers to maintain robust fraud prevention and anti-money laundering (AML) controls.  
It emphasizes compliance with federal, State, and local laws, proper documentation of fraud-related procedures, and proactive risk management processes.

---

## Requirements

1. **Compliance with Laws:**  
   Seller/Servicers must comply with all applicable **federal, State, and local laws**, including statutes related to:
   - Fraud prevention and reporting  
   - Anti-money laundering (AML)  
   - Other financial crime regulations (see Section 1301.2)

2. **Procedural Documentation:**  
   Seller/Servicers must maintain **written procedures** covering:
   - Fraud prevention and detection  
   - Reporting of fraud and suspicious activity  
   - All requirements described in this chapter  
   These procedures must be **made available to Freddie Mac upon request**.

3. **Fraud Risk Management and Monitoring:**  
   Seller/Servicers must implement comprehensive **fraud risk management processes and controls**, which may include:
   - Screening software or automated monitoring tools  
   - Fraud trend analysis and tracking mechanisms  
   - Metrics for monitoring fraud volume, such as:
     - Number of fraud incidents  
     - Number of Exclusionary List matches  
     - Volume of suspicious activity alerts and resolutions  

The goal is to ensure continuous oversight and timely identification of fraud risks across the Seller/Servicer’s mortgage operations.
