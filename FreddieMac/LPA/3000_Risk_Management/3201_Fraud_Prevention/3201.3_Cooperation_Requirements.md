## Overview
This section outlines the **cooperation obligations** of Seller/Servicers with Freddie Mac in connection with the prevention and investigation of fraud and other suspicious activity.  
Seller/Servicers must fully assist Freddie Mac in identifying, verifying, and documenting relevant information about any Mortgage or related real estate transaction under review.

---

## Requirements
Seller/Servicers must:

1. **Cooperate in all investigations** related to fraud, suspected fraud, or other suspicious activity.  
   Cooperation includes making available any **employees or individuals** with direct knowledge of the facts.

2. **Provide requested materials** — when permitted by law — to Freddie Mac upon request, including but not limited to:
   - Closing or settlement agent files  
   - Complete Mortgage files  
   - Borrower payment histories  
   - Re-verifications of employment, income, occupancy, and assets  
   - Any relevant internal or external communications (including emails, notes, or QC findings)

3. **Comply with the 14-day deadline:**  
   All requested information, documentation, and records must be delivered to Freddie Mac **within 14 calendar days** of receiving the request.

Failure to comply with these requirements may result in remedies or enforcement actions under Freddie Mac’s Purchase Documents.
