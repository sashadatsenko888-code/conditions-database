## Overview

Section 3601.1 defines Freddie Mac’s broad authority to enforce remedies against Sellers and Servicers, including repurchase, indemnification, termination of servicing, and other corrective or punitive actions.  
It also explains actions Freddie Mac may take beyond repurchase, and describes obligations triggered by a **Claims Event**, including immediate liability for FRE Claims.

---

# (a) Remedies and Related Requirements

Freddie Mac may exercise remedies **in addition to** any rights under law, equity, or Purchase Documents. For any Mortgage it purchased, Freddie Mac may require the Seller/Servicer to:

### **1. Indemnify Freddie Mac**
- Seller/Servicer must indemnify and hold Freddie Mac harmless  
- Covers loss, damage, expense, court costs, attorney fees

### **2. Mandatory Repurchase – Fraud/Misrepresentation**
Freddie Mac may require repurchase **at any time** if:

- Borrower, or any party to the transaction, made a misstatement, misrepresentation, or omission  
- Applies **regardless** of Seller/Servicer's knowledge or involvement

### **3. Repurchase Under Other Circumstances**
Repurchase or repurchase alternatives may be required under:

- Section **3602.2(a)**  
- Section **3602.2(b)**  

### **4. Termination of Servicing**
- Freddie Mac may terminate the Servicing Contract and Servicing Contract Rights under Chapter 3603

### **5. Setoff Rights**
Freddie Mac may offset any Seller/Servicer obligations using:

- Workout incentives  
- Expense reimbursements  
- Any funds owed by Freddie Mac

---

# (b) Additional Actions Freddie Mac May Take

Beyond remedies above, Freddie Mac may impose additional actions. These include:

### **Operational Oversight & Controls**
- Increased QC or QA reviews  
- Onsite reviews  
- Requests for added data or documentation  
- Require business continuity, remediation, or performance monitoring plans  
- Demand corrective actions or assurances

### **Limitations & Restrictions**
- Limit risk characteristics of Mortgages eligible for sale  
- Limit negotiated business terms  
- Restrict or deny participation in new programs or initiatives  
- Impose additional fees  
- Limit Servicing that can be acquired  
- Limit business volume of Mortgages acquired

### **Personnel & Vendor Actions**
- Add Senior Management to Exclusionary List  
- Require engagement or replacement of Servicing Agents  
- Audit Outsourced Vendors  
- Require voluntary or involuntary Transfer of Servicing

### **Severe Corrective Measures**
- Revoke SHARP designation or servicing rewards  
- Require repurchase of **all Mortgages in default**  
- Suspend the Seller/Servicer  
- Terminate the Seller/Servicer  
- Terminate Servicing Contract, in whole or in part

### **Financial Controls**
- Require posting of collateral (cash or equivalent)  
- Set off or recoup any amount owed by Freddie Mac

---

# (c) Claims Event – Immediate Liability

When a **Claims Event** occurs:

### **1. Immediate Payment Due**
- All FRE Claims become **immediately and automatically due and payable**  
- No further notice or action required

### **2. Condition for Freddie Mac Approval**
To receive Freddie Mac’s approval or consent relating to a Claims Event, Seller/Servicer must:

- Pay all FRE Claims **in full and unconditionally**

### **3. Calculation Finality**
- FRE Claim calculation is **conclusive absent manifest error**  
- Freddie Mac may waive or defer payment in whole or part, at its sole discretion, in writing
