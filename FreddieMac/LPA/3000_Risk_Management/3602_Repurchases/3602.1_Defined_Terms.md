## Overview

Section 3602.1 defines all required terms used in **Chapter 3602 – Repurchases**, including appeal processes, Independent Dispute Resolution (IDR), Servicing violation terms, repurchase demands, and servicing remedies.  
These definitions must be referenced throughout all repurchase-related logic, rules, and automation in ConditionSnap.

---

# Defined Terms

Below are verbatim Guide definitions, organized for clarity.

---

## **A**

### **Appeal process**  
The **first appeal process** and **second appeal process** described in Sections **3602.4(b)** and **3602.4(c)**.

---

## **I**

### **IDR Document**  
A document describing the **Independent Dispute Resolution (IDR) Process**, incorporated into the Guide, available at:  
- https://sf.freddiemac.com/docs/pdf/forms/idr_tolling_agreement.pdf  
- https://sf.freddiemac.com/content/_assets/resources/pdf/step-by-step-guides/serv_success_prog.pdf

### **IDR Eligible Seller/Servicer**  
A Seller/Servicer **not suspended, disqualified, or terminated** by Freddie Mac.  
IDR eligibility **cannot be assigned**.  
A Seller/Servicer is **not IDR eligible** if any of the following apply:

1. Failed to timely comply with an IDR award  
2. Failed to comply with any Repurchase Demand or Servicing Remedy letter after all appeal/escalation options expired  
3. Owes past-due amounts to the **IDR Program Administrator**

### **IDR Process**  
The process described in Sections **3602.4(b)** and **3602.4(c)** and in the IDR Document.

### **IDR Program Administrator**  
The entity designated in the IDR Document.

### **Impasse Process**  
The process described in Sections **3602.4(b)** and **3602.4(c)**.

---

## **M**

### **Management Escalation Process**  
Process described in **3602.4(b)** and **3602.4(c)**.

---

## **R**

### **Repurchase Demand**  
A request issued by Freddie Mac requiring a specific remedy under the Purchase Documents due to an alleged **loan-level breach** of selling representations or warranties.

---

## **S**

### **Servicing correction**  
Action taken by the Servicer showing a Servicing Defect:

- did not exist, **or**
- has been corrected **within Freddie Mac’s timeframe**

### **Servicer Counterparty Status**  
Freddie Mac’s assessment of Servicer’s financial capacity to determine eligibility for certain **Servicing Repurchase Alternatives**.

### **Servicing Defect**  
A loan-level deficiency arising from a **Servicing Violation**, based on a breach of Purchase Document requirements in effect at the time of the violation.

### **Servicing Remedy**  
Action required by Freddie Mac to resolve a Servicing Defect:  
- **Repurchase**, or  
- **Servicing Repurchase Alternative**

### **Servicing Repurchase Alternative**  
A non-repurchase remedy compensating Freddie Mac for losses resulting from a Servicing Defect. May include:

- Daily carrying costs  
- Property taxes, insurance  
- HOA / Coop fees, ground rent  
- Appraisal/BPO costs  
- Legal fees  
- Property inspection fees  
- Utilities  
- Documented property value decline  
- Repairs  
- Outstanding fees, fines, liens  

### **Servicing Repurchase Defect**  
Specific Servicing Defects for which Freddie Mac may issue a **Repurchase Demand immediately**, without prior notice. Includes defects that:

- Subordinate, extinguish or impair Freddie Mac’s lien/interest  
- Pose significant reputational risk  
- Involve improper modification of recourse/indemnification loans  
- Cause the Mortgage to no longer be supported by Freddie Mac systems  
- Cause irreversible physical damage or make the property uninhabitable  

### **Servicing Violation**  
A breach of any Servicer requirement related to:

- Payment processing  
- Delinquency management  
- Borrower communications  
- Loss mitigation  
- Property preservation  
- Required insurance
