## Overview

Section 3402.10 defines the Seller’s **reporting obligations** for quality control (QC) activities, including:

- Internal reporting of QC results to Senior Management  
- Required reporting to Freddie Mac when findings affect eligibility or involve fraud/suspected fraud  

All QC activities—both preclosing and post-closing—must be:

- Fully documented in writing  
- Reviewed regularly by management  

---

# (a) Reporting Results to Senior Management

The Seller must:

### 1. Report All QC Results  
- QC review findings must be reported **in writing**  
- Deadline: **Within 90 days** of the date the Mortgage files were selected for QC review  

### 2. Analyze & Correct  
- Seller must **analyze findings** that affect eligibility or acceptability  
- Take **corrective actions** promptly  

---

# (b) Notification to Freddie Mac

The Seller must notify Freddie Mac when:

### ✔ **Timing requirement**
- Notification must occur **within 30 days** after QC results are reported to Senior Management  
- **Trigger:** Any finding that affects eligibility of a Mortgage sold to Freddie Mac  
- **Also required:** Any fraud or possible fraud finding  

### ✔ **How to notify**
All reportable findings must be submitted via the:

**Tip Referral Tool**  
Accessible through:  
- Freddie Mac Loan Advisor®  
- Freddie Mac Gateway  

---

# Findings That Must Be Reported to Freddie Mac

The Seller must notify Freddie Mac of findings relating to:

### 1. **Fraud, Suspicious Activity, Misrepresentation**
- Misrepresentations, misstatements, omissions per Section 3201.2(a)  
- Any suspected fraud identified in QC or by other means  
- All other suspicious activity must follow Section 3201.2  

### 2. **Borrower Eligibility & Underwriting**
- Borrower creditworthiness  
- Income, credit, liabilities, assets  
- Borrower eligibility (Area Median Income, First-Time Homebuyer, lawful presence)

### 3. **Property / Project Eligibility**
- Residential use compliance  
- Condo eligibility issues  
- Appraisal defects  
- Physical deficiencies in the Mortgaged Premises  

### 4. **Mortgage Terms & Program Criteria**
- LTV, occupancy, credit score requirements  
- Mortgage purpose/product eligibility  
- Refinance limitations  
- Any deviation from negotiated provisions  

### 5. **Life-of-Loan Representations**
- As defined in Section 1301.11  

### 6. **Charter and Legal Matters**
- Charter compliance issues  
- Compliance with laws  

### 7. **Title & Lien Priority**
- First lien issues  
- Clear title failures  

### 8. **Document Execution or Form Errors**
Issues involving the form, sufficiency, or enforceability of Freddie Mac–required documents, including:

- Form 65 (URLA)  
- Power of attorney  
- Texas §50(a)(6) documents  
- Living Trust documents  
- Other nonstandard/special-purpose documents  

### 9. **Insurance Requirements**
- Missing, insufficient, or unenforceable:  
  - Mortgage insurance  
  - Guaranty  
  - Other required insurance  

### 10. **Eligibility at Time of Sale**
Including failures such as:

- Borrower in default at sale  
- Taxes/insurance not paid or escrowed  
- Unauthorized modification, encumbrance, subordination, release  

---

# Freddie Mac Right to Increase Sampling

Freddie Mac may:

- Increase sample size  
- Impose additional requirements  
- Do so **case-by-case** based on QC findings or risk indicators
