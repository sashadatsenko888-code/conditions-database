Section: 3402.11 – Retention of Seller's Quality Control Records  
Effective Date: 04/02/2025

## Overview

Section 3402.11 sets the requirements for how long the Seller must retain **preclosing and post-closing quality control (QC) records**, what must be included in those records, and under what conditions they must be provided to Freddie Mac or a new Servicer.

The purpose is to ensure that Freddie Mac can validate QC activities, confirm Investment Quality Mortgage determinations, and review any loan-level issues when requested.

---

## Record Retention Requirements

The Seller must:

### 1. Retain QC Records for at Least **Three Years**
For a minimum of **three years** from the date of each:

- **Preclosing QC review**, or  
- **Post-closing QC review**

The Seller must retain:

- All QC findings  
- All reverification documentation  
- All corrective action documentation  
- All correspondence or analysis supporting QC conclusions  

---

## Required Availability of Records

The Seller must make retained QC records available:

### 1. **To Freddie Mac**, upon request  
Freddie may request any QC documentation for validation, audit, or enforcement.

### 2. **To a New Servicer**, upon request  
If a Transfer of Servicing occurs, the Seller must provide records to the transferee Servicer.

---

## Required Information When Freddie Mac Requests QC Records

Upon request, the Seller must provide QC records that **clearly show**:

### 1. **Freddie Mac Loan Number**
Must be clearly associated with each QC-reviewed file.

### 2. **Final QC Determination**
The Seller’s QC department must document its final determination of whether the loan is an:

- **Investment Quality Mortgage**

This is a mandatory designation for Freddie Mac validation.

### 3. **Other Loan-Level Information Requested by Freddie Mac**
Freddie may request:

- QC notes  
- Reviewer analysis  
- Documentation lists  
- Reverification results  
- Corrective actions taken  
- Reasons for eligibility or ineligibility determinations  

The Seller must supply all requested data.
